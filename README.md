 Student Performance Analysis

📊 Project Overview
This project analyzes academic performance data for 6,607 students to identify key factors that influence exam scores and performance tiers. The goal was to help educators and institutions make data-driven decisions to improve student outcomes.

---

🛠️ Tools Used
- SQL — data cleaning, filtering, and aggregation
- Power BI — interactive dashboard and data visualization

---

📁 Dataset
- 6,607 student records
- Fields include: Exam Score, Attendance Band, Tutoring Sessions, Performance Tier, and School Type (Private/Public)

---

🔍 Key Findings

- 6,607 students analysed across private and public schools
- Average exam score across all students is 67.24
- High attendance students (90-100%) score an average of 70.14 vs 64.76 for low attendance students — proving attendance directly impacts results
- More tutoring sessions directly correlate with higher exam scores — students with 6 sessions score significantly higher than those with 0
- 21.98% of students are classified as Low Performers
- 53.43% fall in the Medium performance tier and 24.6% in High
- Average tutoring sessions per student is 1.49

---

💡 Recommendations

1. Improve attendance rates — data clearly shows high attendance students score 5+ points higher on average; schools should implement early intervention for students with low attendance
2. Increase access to tutoring — students with more tutoring sessions consistently outperform peers; schools should prioritise making tutoring accessible to all students
3. Target low performers early — with 21.98% of students in the low performance tier, schools need structured support programmes to move these students into the medium tier
